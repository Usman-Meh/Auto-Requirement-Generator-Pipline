Module description
The module focuses on the nature of community practice and the position of occupational therapy within community practice including management perspectives/governance; multi-disciplinary and multi-agency provision and partnership working.Â  Relevant legislation, practice interface and occupational justice/injustices are also considered.
This module is studied by distance learning, delivered online and equates to 200 hours of study. You will be supported by a module tutor via phone, skype and/or email.
Who this course is for
Occupational therapists.
Learning outcomes
On completion of this course you should be able to:
Access critical and informed knowledge of the philosophy of integrating participation and inclusion in community living
Evaluate and critically consider the contribution that occupational therapy can make to the provision of a quality community service
Critically analyse potential future developments
Entry requirements
A qualification in occupational therapy.
