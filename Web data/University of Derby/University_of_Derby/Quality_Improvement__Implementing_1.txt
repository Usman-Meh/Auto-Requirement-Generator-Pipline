Module description
About this module
This module follows on from Quality Improvement- Principles and preparation putting your planned project into action. You will critique the process of implementing your improvement justifying decisions and managing the change, including systems and stakeholders. A core feature of this module is reflective practice, supporting personal and professional development.
On this module you will consider:
Current developments of quality improvement in health and social care
Applying service improvement theories and methodologies to the workplace
Evidence-based practice
Experiencing leadership for service improvement
Experiencing entrepreneurial and creativity qualities
Undertaking project management and strategic planning for service improvement
Employing change management principles (personal, professional and organisational)
Undertaking evaluation strategies
Measuring improvement in the workplace
Engaging with stakeholders
Working with teams.
Who is this course for
All health professionals
On completion of this course you should be able to:
Critically evaluate the courses of action relating to implementing quality improvement taking into account issues beyond the immediate area of practice.
Manage quality improvement activities effectively in the workplace, responding to value-conflicts and uncertainties which extend beyond the immediate area of practice.
Demonstrate innovation and strategic thinking in the implementation of service improvement activities.
Learning outcomes
On completion of this course you should be able to:
Employ thought-through courses of action relating to implementing service improvement taking into account issues beyond the immediate area of practice.
Manage service improvement activities effectively in the workplace, responding to value-conflicts and uncertainties which extend beyond the immediate area of practice.
Demonstrate innovation and strategic thinking in the implementation of service improvement activities.
