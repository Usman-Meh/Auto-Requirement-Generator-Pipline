Overview
The Master’s Degree in
Robotics, Automation and Electrical Engineering
aims to train high-level technicians and professionals equipped with a significant mastery of analytical and numerical modeling methods and general technical-scientific content of Robotics, Automation and Electrical Engineering, with a marked interdisciplinary attitude and providing the knowledge, skills and abilities that are the basis of numerous “Jobs of the future,” so as to meet the very strong demand from the labor market.
The master’s degree graduate in
Robotics, Automation and Electrical Engineering
is a professional figure with a solid advanced background and capable of filling technical roles in the development, design and management of automated, robotic, mechatronic and enertronic systems in various fields such as industrial automation and robotics, process industry, collaborative industry electrical power generation, transmission and distribution systems and networks, renewable energy sources, power electronic circuits, electrical machines and drives, smart grids, sustainable electrical design, diagnostics, risk and safety assessment, integrating measurement, generation, conversion, transmission and actuation components and equipment. be played.
Career opportunities
Regardless of which of the two possible paths is chosen (Robotics and Automation or Electrical Engineering), the master’s degree graduate in Robotics, Automation and Electrical Engineering, also thanks to his or her interdisciplinary preparation, possesses the requirements of an expert in the study, design, development, production, industrialization and management of complex and/or innovative systems with high technological content and is able to deal with the resolution of complex problems both independently and by interacting effectively with professional figures from different backgrounds.
In addition, the master’s degree program in Robotics, Automation and Electrical Engineering provides access to the qualifying examination for the practice of engineering in Italy in Section A (persons holding a master’s or specialist or five-year single-cycle degree) in both the field of Information Engineering and the field of Industrial Engineering.
Finally, possible outlets also include the two levels of higher education, namely second-level master’s degree and PhD, as well as technical careers in universities and research institutions and teaching in secondary schools.
Three professional profiles are foreseen:
Profile 1: Robotics and Automation Engineer
Profile 2: Electrical Power Engineer
Profile 3: Engineer with expertise in commissioning, safety, and certification of systems and plants
