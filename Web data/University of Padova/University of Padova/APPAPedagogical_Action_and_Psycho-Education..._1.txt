APPA - Pedagogical Action and Psycho-Educational Interventions to Promote the Adult Life of People with Autism
The
Post-Graduate Course in Pedagogical Action and Psycho-
Educational Interventions to Promote the Adult Life of People with Autism
provides the necessary skills to follow the people with autism and their families in an independent adult life project, aimed at pursuing social inclusion objectives. The main targer includes those subjects that, at the end of the school years, are at risk of experimenting situations of social isolation or exclusion.
The main educational objectives include the
acquisition of specific skills and competences to promote educational interventions addressed to people with autism
, with the aim of building a life project capable of including those people in the reference community, increase independent life skills (social, relational, communicational, work-related), promote new synergies and collaborations between professionals, by identifying the possible connections with the resources and the opportunities in the relevant territories, through enabling co-projects.
Learning content
The
Post-Graduate Course in Pedagogical Action and Psycho-
Educational Interventions to Promote the Adult Life of People with Autism
provides healthcare operators with a deep knowledge and understanding of the following topics:
pedagogical approach to disabilities;
pedagogical prognosis in reference to the diagnosis, and functional profiles of people with autism;
healthcare and welfare models, and the main pedagogical action and psycho-educational intervention theories and principles;
communication and languages, the corporeal, sensory and physical dimension in adult life;
soft skills and orientation paths in school;
adult life within the life cycle; co-creation of an ICF-based life project.
The course will be provided in blended mode, with 50% in-classroom lessons and 50% remote classes. In-classroom lessons will be held one afternoon a week.
The course comprises lessons, remote learning, individual study, a project work and final exam. There will be a final exam for all modules.
Recipients and career opportunities
The
Post-Graduate Course in Pedagogical Action and Psycho-
Educational Interventions to Promote the Adult Life of People with Autism
trains professional figures, such as regional healthcare service professionals; some of its objectives include the ability to apply specific knowledge to promote educational interventions addressed to people with autism, with the
goal of creating a life project
that includes the person in the reference community; increase the ability to apply pedagogical mediation to support independent life in people with autism, by increasing the chances of developing their social-relational, communicational and work-related skills; promoting new synergies and collaborations between professionals, by identifying possible connections with local resources and opportunities, through enabling co-projects.
Program
The
Post-Graduate Course in Pedagogical Action and Psycho-Educational Interventions to Promote the Adult Life of People with Autism
provides specific knowledge on the following areas and topics:
PEDAGOGICAL AREA
ICF-BASED MULTIDIMENSIONAL ASSESSMENT
Analysis of the bio-psycho-social function through the ICF
MEDICAL AREA
CHARACTERISTIC DIAGNOSIS – FUNCTIONAL PROFILES
Neuro-psychological functional profiles and comorbidity in adult people with autism
Functional assessment and main intervention models
MEDICAL AREA
COMMUNICATION AND LANGUAGE
Communicative and linguistic characteristics of people with autism
Alternative augmentative communication
PEDAGOGICAL AREA
SCHOOL: FROM THE EIP TO A LIFE PROJECT
The National EIP and the GLO, transition models toward adult life: a comparison of the paradigms and evaluation matters.
The PCTO as tool for the PDV of students with autism
Dialogues on the future: cultivating a (dis)orientation between parents and students
PEDAGOGICAL AREA
A PERSONALIZED AND PARTICIPATED INDIVIDUAL LIFE PROJECT
Rules on the life project The life project from a pedagogical standpoint
PSYCHO-EDUCATIONAL AREA
PRE-ADOLESCENCE AND ADOLESCENCE: MODELS/SUPPORT METHODS DURING PRE-ADOLESCENCE AND ADOLESCENCE WITHIN THE LIFECYCLE OF A PERSON WITH AUTISM
Autism and adolescence: psychological analysis
Autism and adolescence: educational matters
PEDAGOGICAL AREA
ADULT LIFE: MODELS/SUPPORT METHODS DURING ADULTHOOD WITHIN THE LIFECYCLE OF A PERSON WITH AUTISM
Self-determination between identity and community
Communication and educational relationship
Work and working life
Relationships, emotional and sexual life
Caring citizenship (CBR and community welfare)
PEDAGOGICAL AREA
LEGAL SYSTEMS FOR THE PROTECTION OF VULNERABLE SUBJECTS
