Creative writing
Choose a major in Creative Writing
Your story has just begun.
Stories are an integral part of being human. They tell our history, aspirations, ideas and emotions. With a major in Creative Writing, you’ll learn to write in a range of genres and modes, making use of different mediums and technology. You’re sure to find your voice as you develop a portfolio work—practising your new skills and receiving feedback from your peers and published, award-winning teachers. By the time you graduate, you’ll be ready for an exciting career in the arts and communication industries.
Why study Creative Writing?
Be mentored and inspired by published, award-winning writers
Explore a range of genres, and discover new strengths in different writing styles
Choose from a broad range of topics including literary studies, fiction and editing
Learn to design work for a range of audiences in relevant formats
Prepare for the workforce with highly desirable skills in communication and creative intelligence
What you will study
Literary fiction, poetry, life writing and genre fiction
Publishing and editing
Academic and professional communication
Gothic tradition and medieval myths

How many units?
A major in Creative Writing is made up of 36 units, including:
two Year 1 topics (9 units)
two Year 2 topics (9 units)
two Year 3 topics (9 units)
plus two additional topics (9 units) from the Year 2 or Year 3 topics.
Creative Writing can also be taken as a minor. Your 22.5 units will include:
two Year 1 topics (9 units)
two Year 2 topics (9 units)
plus an additional topic (4.5 units) from the Year 2 or Year 3 topics.
Creative Writing is not offered as an extended major.

Your career
Creative writing develops the creative, practical, critical and collaborative skills necessary to pursue a career in the communications, arts and related creative industries.
Potential occupations include:
freelance writer
marketing and communication
publishing copyeditor or proofreader
web content manager.
Potential employers include:
publishing companies
marketing agencies or departments
newspapers, magazines and other media production companies
education institutions
the arts industry.
