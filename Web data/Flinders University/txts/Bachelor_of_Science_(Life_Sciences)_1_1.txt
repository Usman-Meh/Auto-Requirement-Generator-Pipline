Bachelor of Science (Life Sciences)
Study Life Sciences at Flinders
Life finds a way. Study the environmental, biological, medical and scientific basis of life.
Explore the importance of biological processes on human health and environmental well-being. This degree combines aspects of molecular biology, cell physiology and microbiology
with new computational approaches to discover fundamental aspects of life. The course also provides students the opportunity to explore areas of biotechnology, forensic science, plant science, environmental health, and biomedical sciences.
You will graduate well-prepared for a career in any life-science related field.

Why study Life Sciences at Flinders?
The Bachelor of Science (Life Sciences) degree provides you with broad career opportunities.
You will develop applied and job-ready skills through comprehensive laboratory practicals that are embedded in many of the topics.
Completion of the Bachelor of Science (Life Sciences) degree forms an excellent foundation for Honours/PhD studies or transitioning to a graduate medicine degree.
You will integrate big data processing skills that are translatable to many different fields, even outside of science.
Our modern laboratories allow you to excel in various cutting-edge technologies.
Your career
A life sciences degree will prepare you for a broad range of industries and an almost infinite amount of opportunities.
Potential occupations include:
Clinical diagnostics
Quality control (e.g. pharmaceuticals)
Various roles in the food innovation industry
Biotechnologist
Researcher within the molecular life sciences industry
Potential employers include:
Government (e. Department of Health and Wellbeing or Environment Protection Authority)
Diverse pathology services
SA Water
Food technology companies
What you will study
First year
A typical first year may include:
In your first year you will gain core knowledge in the molecular basis of life, evolution and chemistry, by combining laboratory-based practical sessions with problem-based learning exercises and workshop-assisted theory.
Second year
A typical second year may include:
In addition to expanding your molecular and biochemical knowledge base, in the second year of study you will integrate your choice of applied life sciences (medical, plant and/or microbiology) and develop new skills in approaching scientific problems in innovative ways.
Third year
A typical third year may include:
In the third year of study, you will combine research-driven knowledge, with real-world work simulations in collaborative projects. This will prepare you for entering the job market whilst simultaneously laying an outstanding foundation for further academic challenges such as Honours and PhD studies.
Practical experience
Our topics places a major emphasis on gaining practical skills, to apply your new knowledge and develop a comprehensive understanding of the molecular basis of life.

If you don’t meet our English language entry requirements and need to improve your English language proficiency, you can do so through Flinders University Academy – or our approved English Language Instruction Course for Overseas Students (ELICOS) providers.
This means that you can attend the required English language tuition at approved ELICOS providers and gain direct entry into university without an IELTS or TOEFL test.
