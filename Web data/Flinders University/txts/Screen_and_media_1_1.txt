Screen and media
Choose a major in Screen and Media
Add to your story.
Screen and media provides you with a chance to study screen-based media such as film, television and digital technologies, including social media, gaming and 3D visualisation. You’ll explore how commercial and aesthetic factors, cultural and social forces, and historical and theoretical perspectives shape media and contribute to our understandings and interactions with them. Try your hand at video, production, directing, scriptwriting, post-production, interactive design and graphic design in one of many practical topics.
Why study Screen and Media?
Study the aesthetics, histories and theories associated with a wide range of media
Enhance your critical thinking skills through your study of media objects, industries, and practices
Learn about the important roles media takes up in society and culture
Become a critical and creative consumer
Acquire the skills employers value: clear communication, critical thinking, and collaboration
What you will study
Television, film, social media and gaming
Popular genres and non-fiction
Media industries in global and local contexts
Australian/Indigenous media
Narrative and storytelling
Interactive design, virtual reality and technologies of visualisation
Gender and cross-cultural media
Media policy and ethics
Children’s media

How many units?
A major in Screen and Media is made up of 36 units, including:
two Year 1 topics (9 units)
two Year 2 topics (9 units)
two Year 3 topics (9 units)
plus two additional topics (9 units) from the Year 2 or Year 3 topics.
Screen and Media can also be taken as a minor. Your 22.5 units will include:
two Year 1 topics (9 units)
two Year 2 topics (9 units)
plus an additional topic (4.5 units) from the Year 2 or Year 3 topics.
You may complete an extended major by completing the prescribed Screen and Media major topics (36 units), plus an additional two topics (9 units) from Year 2 or Year 3.

Potential employers include:
large corporations and small to medium businesses
national, state, and local government and agencies
media and creative industry sectors
tech and social media start-ups
other media production companies.
