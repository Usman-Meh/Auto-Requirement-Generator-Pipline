Ecology and evolutionary biology
Choose a major in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology
Evolve your knowledge
Ecology and Evolutionary Biology focuses on the study of living animals, plants and fungi, how they interact, and their role in the complex ecology of life in communities. Explore your love of nature and grow your understanding of the evolution and ecology of biodiversity in Australia and around the world. How does life on Earth respond to changing conditions? How are humans impacting on the environment and what can we do to live sustainably within our ecosystems? How can you lend your knowledge to the environmental challenges of tomorrow?
Why study  Ecology and Evolutionary Biology?
Develop the skills and knowledge to help you protect the environment
Explore how the diversity of life on earth has evolved
Learn how living species interact in communities and ecosystems
Use scientific approaches to solving environmental problems
Develop laboratory and field skills in ecology
What you will study
Evolution, genetics and biological diversity
Molecular science
Ecology
Biostatistics
Chemistry, chemical structure and bonding
Experimental design
Conservation biology and restoration ecology
Palaeontology

Your career
Graduate ready to pursue a career in ecology, animal behaviour, environmental management or consulting, conservation, science writing, policy, or ecotourism.
Potential occupations include:
biologist
ecologist
environmental planner, officer or consultant
environmental education officer.
Potential employers include:
zoos and wildlife sanctuaries
environmental and conservation organisations
government bodies.
