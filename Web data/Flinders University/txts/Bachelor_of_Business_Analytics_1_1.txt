Study Business Analytics at Flinders
Transform data into decisions with a Bachelor of Business Analytics, designed to equip you with cutting-edge tools and techniques.
Want to know how data can help businesses succeed? With the Bachelor of Business Analytics, you’ll learn how to turn data into insights for big questions.
You’ll become skilled in analysing data, predicting trends, and managing information, all while working on analysis tools used by professionals and on hands-on projects.
Whether you’re interested in business, healthcare, finance, or technology, this course sets you up for exciting careers where you’ll use data to make smart decisions and help companies grow.

Why study Business Analytics at Flinders
This degree provides participants with practical commercial skills to interpret data and information to drive organisational value and gain a competitive edge
You will learn how to lead with data-driven confidence and obtain a strategic mindset
You will use analytics tools and techniques like machine learning models and integrate artificial intelligence used by professionals
Work on real-world projects and obtain industry experience to analyse future trends and outcomes and grow your professional network
The course is offered in person at Bedford Park or the Flinders’ city campus.

What you will study
First year
A typical first year may include:
First-year studies focus on foundational knowledge in business and computing principles, and an introduction to analytics tools. Topics include: Harnessing the Power of Fintech, Fundamentals in Computing, Interpreting and Visualising Business Data, Finance and Accounting for Leaders.
Second year
A typical second year may include:
In the second year, students delve into more advanced analytics tools and techniques, ethics in data use, and option to take a minor in Data Analytics or 2 electives from across the university. Topics include: Uncovering Global Market Insights, Database Modelling and Information Management, HR Information Systems & Analytics, Business Forecasting.
Third year
A typical third year may include:
The final year emphasises strategic decision-making using analytics, projects reflecting real-world scenarios, an industry placement or project, and further specialisation through a minor or an elective. Topics include: Digital Marketing & Analysis, Supply Chain Analytics, and AI, Cybersecurity and the Future of Work.

Practical experience
The degree provides you with practical experience that prepares you for the workforce, including an industry placement or industry project where you will have the opportunity to apply knowledge, practice skills and build networks in the industry.
Study abroad
Flinders has a series of international exchange links that give you a chance to study part of your degree overseas, from a short-term three-week study tour to a yearlong exchange at any one of 80 partner universities around the world.
