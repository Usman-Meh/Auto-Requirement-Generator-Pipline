Remote Health Practice
Overview
The Remote Health Practice program aims to meet the higher education needs of health professionals working in remote areas and supports the transition to remote practice for those who have an interest in joining the remote health workforce.
Health professionals are challenged to advance their knowledge of the discipline of remote health and develop the necessary skills for working within the remote and Indigenous context to improve health outcomes of remote and Indigenous populations.
This articulated program was developed in collaboration with CRANAplus and is offered in online mode through Flinders University, Alice Springs campus.

What you will study
Remote Health Practice degrees prepare health professionals new to remote contexts to keep remote, regional and Indigenous people well and manage ill-health. They aim to provide education at a foundation level across three major content areas that underpin practice within the discipline of remote health locally, nationally and internationally: the remote context, public health, and application to practice.

Entry requirements
Graduate Certificate
Applicants must normally hold an approved degree or equivalent qualification in a health-related field.
Graduate Diploma
Applicants must normally hold an approved degree or equivalent qualification in a health-related field, or the Graduate Certificate in Remote Health Practice.
Masters
Applicants must normally hold a bachelor degree in a health-related field, and have three years' full-time equivalent work experience in a health-related field, or hold the Graduate Diploma in Remote Health Practice.
