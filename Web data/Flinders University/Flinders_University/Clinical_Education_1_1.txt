Clinical Education
Are you a health professional interested in deepening and expanding your educational practice?
Our Clinical Education courses, provide you with advanced knowledge and skills to deliver high-quality education, evaluate and improve learning and teaching, undertake clinical education research, and lead education within a health service and social care settings.
Students will apply evidence to local educational practices and processes, as a basis for improvement and change, delivering direct benefit to you, your students and industry.
Advance your career - leave a legacy in your health profession.

Your career
Graduates are able to contribute to the education profession in today’s dynamic and demanding health service settings through new and innovative approaches to clinical education. They are suitable for careers in clinical teaching in the government and private health sectors.
Potential employers include:
hospitals
government departments
private health organisations.
What you will study
The Clinical Education postgraduate courses at Flinders support health and social care practitioners to advance their expertise in education.
The Graduate Certificate (4 topics) has an applied focus on improving delivery of health and clinical education in a range of settings
The Graduate Diploma (8 topics) has a focus on building knowledge and skills to evaluate and improve health and clinical education
The Master of Clinical Education (Coursework) (12 topics) builds on the first two awards, and supports health professionals to lead health and clinical education in a range of environments and systems
The Master of Clinical Education (Research) is a higher degree by research program aimed exclusively at health practitioners who want to contribute to this specialist body of knowledge by undertaking a significant piece of educational research.

Entry requirements
Graduate Certificate
Applicants must normally hold a bachelor degree or equivalent qualification and have at least six months full-time equivalent of work experience in a clinical or health related field such as; but not limited to; nursing, midwifery, medicine or health therapy.
Graduate Diploma
Applicants must normally hold a bachelor degree or equivalent qualification or equivalent qualification and have at least one year full-time equivalent work experience in a clinical or health related field such as nursing, midwifery, medicine or health therapy; or hold a Graduate Certificate in Clinical Education.
Masters
Applicants must normally hold a bachelor degree or equivalent qualification and must have at least one and a half years of full-time equivalent work experience in a clinical or health related field such as; but not limited to; nursing, midwifery, medicine or health therapy; or hold the Graduate Diploma in Clinical Education or equivalent qualification.

Entry requirements
Graduate Certificate
Applicants must normally hold an approved degree or equivalent qualification from an approved tertiary institution.
Graduate Diploma
Applicants must normally hold an approved degree or equivalent qualification from an approved tertiary institution, or the Graduate Certificate in Clinical Education, or equivalent.
Masters
Applicants for the Master of Clinical Education who do not hold the Graduate Certificate in Clinical Education or the Graduate Diploma in Clinical Education must normally hold an approved degree or equivalent qualification from an approved tertiary institution and have not less than two years’ work experience in a relevant clinical area.
