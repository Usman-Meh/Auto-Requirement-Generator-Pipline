Choose a major in Languages
Study that speaks to your interests.
Learn to speak and read other languages, delve into other cultures and build a global understanding of the world. With the internationalisation of the job market, graduates with a qualification in a foreign language will have greater employment opportunities both in Australia and overseas. Studying a language is a multidisciplinary pursuit. You will improve your communication skills, enhance your sociocultural understanding of local and international environments, and gain insights into diverse cultural and linguistic practices.

How many topics will you study?
A language major is made up of 36 units, including:
two Year 1 topics (9 units)
two Year 2 topics (9 units)
two Year 3 topics (9 units)
plus two additional topics (9 units) from the Year 2 or Year 3 topics.
Languages can also be taken as a minor. Your 22.5 units will include:
two Year 1 topics (9 units)
two Year 2 topics (9 units)
plus an additional topic (4.5 units) from the Year 2  or Year 3 topics.
Languages are only offered as extended majors if you are enrolled in the Bachelor of Languages.
