Learn from renowned researchers in a range of sciences including biotechnology, natural sciences, mathematics and chemical and physical sciences. You will complete an independent research project, learn new skills and help bring important scientific ideas into reality.

Why undertake an Honours year in Science at Flinders?
Question, problem solve and experiment with science
Expand your area of expertise
Obtain practical skills in laboratory and field techniques and scientific communication
Lead an independent research project to obtain new knowledge
Learn from world-leading researchers in your area of interest
Establish links with research students, scholars and industry contacts
Prepare for postgraduate studies, research higher degree or doctorate

What you will study
You can complete your Honours year studies in a range of science disciplines. Depending on your area of interest, expand your advance understanding and gain valuable skills in reviewing literature, planning and executing experiments, time management, organisational skills and science communication and presentations.
Areas of specialisation:
Biological Sciences
Chemical and Physical Sciences
Environment
Biotechnology
Computer Science
Mathematics and Statistics.

Entry requirements
Entry into one-year Honours is highly competitive. If you have demonstrated academic excellence (a Grade Point Average of 5.25 or above) during your bachelor degree you are invited to apply for Honours.
Whether you’re invited to apply, or want to apply, you will need to contact the Honours coordinator in your discipline to discuss your project and arrange for supervision.

How to apply
Submit your application directly to Flinders University through the online application system.
If you have never studied at or made an application to Flinders University
, you can register and submit your application for admission to a 1-year Honours course using the
eApplication portal
. You will receive two emails once your application is completed: confirmation of application submission; and a second email with your student ID. Note, it may take 1-4 hours for your student ID to be created.
If you have studied at Flinders University within the last 12 months
you can submit your application for admission to a one-year Honours course via the
Student System
. You can apply for admission by selecting the
My Applications
tab at the top of the page, and searching for your course.
If you studied at Flinders University more than 12 months ago
you will need to
reactivate your FAN
first. With an active FAN you will then be able to submit your application for admission via the
Student System
as advised above.
