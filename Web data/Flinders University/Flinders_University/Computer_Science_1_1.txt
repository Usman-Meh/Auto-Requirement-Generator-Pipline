Flinders postgraduate Computer Science courses are an opportunity for people with a computer science, software/computer systems engineering, information technology, information systems or equivalent background to prepare themselves to tackle some of the biggest technological challenges of the 21
st
century.
Your career
A practical, industry-linked degree, Flinders postgraduate computer science courses will see you graduate not just with the skills to
seek
a career, but fully prepared to step into one.
Advanced computer science qualifications such as Flinders Master of Computer Science are in high demand in telecommunications, aerospace and defence, government, higher education and private organisations.

Professional accreditation/recognition:
The course is accredited by the Australian Computer Society at the level of professional. The course accreditation is recognised internationally for entry to professional practice by seven other accrediting bodies through the Seoul Accord.

What you will study
Study the theory and practical aspects of topics as diverse as computer programming, intelligent and interactive systems, digital media, robotics, systems engineering, heuristic optimisation and more. You’ll work in close consultation with industry, building scientific skills that are in demand and up-to-the-minute, and running industry-relevant projects.
You won’t just study the science, you’ll DO the science in a real-world environment.
Practical experience
The Master of Computer Science involves significant project work, notably a major individual project that may tackle an industry-relevant problem or a research question.
