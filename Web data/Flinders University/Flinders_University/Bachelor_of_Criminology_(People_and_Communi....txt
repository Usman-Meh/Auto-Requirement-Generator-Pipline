Bachelor of Criminology (People and Community)
Study Criminology (People and Community) at Flinders
Learn how to increase your positive social impact and work with clients, their families and communities impacted by the criminal justice system.
Push the boundaries and learn to work directly with people impacted by the criminal justice system.
With a focus on working with people impacted by crime, this criminology specialisation provides a comprehensive understanding of the impacts of criminalisation and victimisation, preparing you for careers in community and social justice sectors.
By undertaking a selection of topics from psychology, disability studies, and social work you will learn how to apply your knowledge and skills in a person-focused context, and future-proof your career in an ever-changing work landscape.

Why study Criminology (People and Community) at Flinders
Learn from South Australia’s largest and most experienced criminology department
Gain a unique person -focused lens to studying criminology, that provides practical skills enabling you to work face-to-face with groups or individuals to solve problems and help manage their future direction
Develop in depth understanding from a range of disciplines including psychology, social work and business, setting you up with holistic skills that will set you apart
Health and Welfare Services Managers jobs are set to grow by 30% in the ten years to May 2033 (Source: Jobs and Skills Australia 2024)
Complete an industry placement and connect with leaders, Flinders Criminology has powerful industry partnerships spanning government bodies, public and private organisations
Your career
This degree will prepare you for a career working with clients, families and those impacted by crime, setting you up for a career in high demand.
Potential occupations include:
Youth worker

Victim support officer

Law enforcement and corrections agencies
What you will study
First year
A typical first year may include:
Foundational topics in criminology, introducing areas such as criminal law, the criminal justice system, crime data, .crime and criminology, and race and representation
Second year
A typical second year may include:
Enhanced topics building on first year that explore policing, the social impacts of punishment, cybercrime and digital risk, and victims and trauma
Specialised topics in children, young people and the justice system, groups and communities
Third year
A typical third year may include:
Specialised topics in counselling, building community capacity
A choice of elective topics providing enhanced understanding of areas such as international criminal justice, current issues in criminal justice, drugs politics and public health, and others
