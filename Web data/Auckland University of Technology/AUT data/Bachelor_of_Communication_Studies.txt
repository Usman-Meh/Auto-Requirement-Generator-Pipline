That’s because our students pick up top jobs in the best companies and media outlets, and win many prestigious awards
AUT communications students and graduates are internationally recognised, having won the Sally Logan-Milne Young Practitioner of the Year award, CAANZ AXIS award, ORCA awards, Cannes International Advertising award and NZ Student Marketer of the Year award
Study communications at AUT and you have access to industry standard facilities and equipment, including a media centre, radio studio and fully equipped TV studio, journalism training newscourse Te Waha Nui, student radio station Static 88.1 fm and student-run PR agency Outside the Square
You also gain invaluable workplace experience before you graduate

To graduate with a Bachelor of Communication Studies you need to complete: Core courses (120 points) These are courses all students in this degree need to take in the first year
They give you a basic understanding of the media world and communication theories, and help you decide which subject to focus on later in your studies
Your chosen major (120 points) Your major is the subject area you want to specialise in
This makes up one third of your degree, and usually consists of eight courses related to your chosen subject
You can view the list of majors in this degree in the ‘Majors’ tab
Flexible component (120 points) You can choose one of the following options Two minors (60 points each); or A minor (60 points) and elective courses (60 points); or A second major (120 points) Your second major, minor(s) and elective courses can be from different AUT degrees; they don’t need to be from communication studies
