Our visual arts degree prepares you for the demands of a career in contemporary art, whether as an artist or in the many other roles within the art sector
Study visual arts and enjoy a student-centred and studio-based programme where you can follow your interests in art – including painting, printmaking, drawing, photography, sculpture or moving-image
As a graduate of our visual arts programme, you’ll have a sound understanding of the trends and concepts driving contemporary art, the importance of mātauranga Māori, and how to work collaboratively to produce objects, craft, images, taonga and artefacts

Email artbox@aut.ac.nz As a visual arts student, you can pursue your passion for art, experiment with different techniques and materials, and prepare yourself for a future in the visual arts
If you started in 2024 or are starting in 2025 You can build your degree in a way that suits you and your interests, and include subjects from outside of visual arts as part of your studies
To graduate with this degree you need to complete: Core courses (120 points) These are courses all students in this degree need to take
They cover fundamental visual arts principles and processes, and help you decide which subject to focus on later in your studies
Two of the core courses are the courses Integrated Visual Arts Practices and Professional Contexts in your final semester, which give you a chance to apply what you’ve learnt in a practice-based project and further your understanding of what to expect from a career in the art industry
Your chosen major (120 points) Your major is the subject area you want to specialise in
This makes up one third of your degree and consists of courses related to your chosen subject
You can view the list of majors in this degree in the ‘Majors’ tab
Flexible component (120 points) You can choose one of the following options Two minors (60 points each); or A minor (60 points) and elective courses (60 points); or A second major (120 points) Your second major, minor(s) and elective courses can be from different AUT degrees; they don’t need to be from visual arts
