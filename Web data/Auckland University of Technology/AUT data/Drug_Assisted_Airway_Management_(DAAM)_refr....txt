Study Menu In this section Home Study Study options Study health sciences Courses Drug Assisted Airway Management (DAAM) refresher short course Drug Assisted Airway Management (DAAM) refresher short course This short course is designed to refresh your knowledge and practice in drug assisted airway management
It’s run by the AUT paramedicine team in conjunction with industry
Course overview Modules cover pharmacology, DAAM procedures, difficult airways and mechanical ventilation
You’ll also participate in skill stations, clinical simulations and case study discussions
About this course Classes How to apply What you’ll study in the DAAM course Online modules There are four online modules in the form of multimedia presentations covering: Pharmacology DAAM procedures Recognition and troubleshooting the difficult airway Review of mechanical ventilation This culminates in a compulsory formative MCQ assessment
The classroom-based session includes: Skill stations Ventilator troubleshooting Airway assessment Reasoning and rationale for RSI/DSI/truncated intubation Cricothyroidotomy Inotropic support Clinical simulations Case study discussions How long this course will take This course should take approximately 32 hours of online learning and an 8-hour classroom session
You’ll have 2 months to complete the online learning prior to attending class
You’ll need to enrol to gain access to the online learning
Who this course is suitable for This short course is primarily aimed at the prehospital emergency services, intensive care paramedics with rapid sequence intubation endorsement and those wishing to challenge for this endorsement
It’s been designed with working health professionals in mind
The pre-learning through the AUT online Canvas platform is designed to be a self-paced programme that you can progress through at your own speed, place and time with only a one day in-class component
