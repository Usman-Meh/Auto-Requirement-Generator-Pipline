Study Menu In this section Home Study Study options Study health sciences Courses Prehospital Antenatal Care online short course Prehospital Antenatal Care online short course This course is designed to refresh your knowledge and practice in antenatal care in the prehospital setting
It is run by our paramedicine team in conjunction with the industry
Course overview There are 4 online modules in the form of multimedia presentations covering physiology and assessment and normal delivery, antenatal complications, complex delivery, and neonatal resuscitation and care
About this course Classes How to apply What you’ll study As well as the multimedia presentations, additional content will also be provided in the form of articles, reviews, studies and multimedia videos to enhance your learning and improve your practice
Assessments The online short course culminates in four compulsory formative MCQ assessments, at the end of each module
How long this course will take The course should take approximately 16 hours of online learning; however, you have 6 months to complete the online learning
You'll need to enrol to gain access to the online learning
Who this course is suitable for This short course is primarily aimed at the prehospital emergency services: intensive care paramedics, paramedics and EMTs who have completed a Bachelor of Health Science in Paramedicine or those that have an Authority to Practice (ATP) wishing to remain current with their clinical practice
It has been designed with working health professionals in mind
The pre-learning through the AUT online Canvas platform is designed to be a self-paced programme that you can progress through at your own speed, place and time
There are no onsite based classroom requirements
This course is open to domestic and international students
