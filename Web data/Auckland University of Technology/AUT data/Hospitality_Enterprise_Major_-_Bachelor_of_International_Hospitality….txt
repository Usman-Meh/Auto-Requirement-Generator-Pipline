The Hospitality Enterprise major in the Bachelor of International Hospitality Management focuses on the development of hospitable employee and customer experiences
You’ll explore strategies to improve staff wellbeing and propose innovative ways to create sustainable futures for the hospitality industry
Studying hospitality offers a lot of flexibility, and you can include subjects from other AUT degrees as part of your studies

Year 1 You take two core courses that give you a fundamental understanding of applied food and beverage management, and international hospitality management
You’re also introduced to human resource management in hospitality, human and organisational behaviour, and social enterprise and innovation in hospitality
You study the following courses: Core courses HOSP502 Applied Food and Beverage Management (Restaurant) HOSP507 International Hospitality Management Hospitality enterprise courses HOSP501 Applied Food and Beverage Management (Kitchen) HOSP509 Hospitality Social Enterprise HOSP604 Hospitality Human Resource Management HOSP609 Hospitality Innovation and Change In your second semester, you also take the first two courses from your second major, minor(s) or elective courses
Year 2 This year you explore critical and ethical perspectives of hospitality and its role in society, service design and how to create an excellent guest experience
You study the following courses: Core course HOSP611 Critical Hospitality Perspectives Hospitality enterprise courses HOSP615 Service Design Thinking HOSP717 Guest Experience MGMT721 Leadership Principles, Perspectives and Practice You also continue to take courses from your second major, minor(s) or elective courses
Year 3 You further your knowledge of people management in hospitality and tourism, leadership principles and innovation in hospitality
You study the following courses: Core course HOSP716 Hospitality Innovation Competition Hospitality enterprise course HOSP715 Holistic People Management for Hospitality and Tourism You also take the final courses from your second major, minor(s) or elective courses
Workplace experience In your final semester, you complete valuable workplace experience with an organisation (this is the Hospitality and Tourism Work Integrated Learning course)
It's a great opportunity to develop industry networks and make a decision on your professional career after graduation
