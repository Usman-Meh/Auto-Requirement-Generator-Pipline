Science is everywhere. A general science degree provides a comprehensive education in science, exposing you to a wide range of disciplines. You can explore what interests you most while gaining a strong foundation in mathematics and computational sciences, ensuring you're equipped to meet the most in-demand skills.
Choose from a range of courses to attain a general science education. Complete two of the following six concentrations to earn your degree:
Biology
Learn about the science of life in one of three areas: anatomy and physiology, cellular and molecular biology, or ecology and evolution.
Chemistry
Get a mix of classroom and practical laboratory instruction with a focus on organic chemistry, biochemistry and molecular structures.
Geography
Take courses in physical geography and select additional courses from fields of biogeography, sustainable development, mapping and geographic information systems.
Geology
Explore different aspects of geology through courses in physical geology, paleontology, hydrogeology and others.
Mathematics
Build the tools you will need to understand the mathematical basis of all sciences. Take courses in algebra, calculus, modelling and statistics.
Physics
Explore waves and optics, electrodynamics, thermodynamics and quantum mechanics.
